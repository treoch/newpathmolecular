Our approach

Synthetic chemistry where biologics cannot go

Phosphatidylserine is exposed on the outer membrane of tumour vasculature across tumour types. It presents a targeting challenge that antibodies and small molecules cannot solve — which is exactly where our platform operates.

01

A conserved, pan-tumour biomarker

Phosphatidylserine (PS) flips to the outer leaflet of cancer cell membranes and tumour vasculature across tumour types — regardless of the mutations driving each tumour. Multiple independent research groups have replicated this finding.

02

A modality gap that biologics can't close

PS has no accessible direct binding site for antibodies, and no conventional binding pocket for small molecules. Neither existing modality can efficiently exploit it — leaving the target under-addressed despite its broad exposure.

03

Solved via synthetic chemistry

New Path's proprietary platform builds synthetic peptide-drug conjugates (PDCs) engineered to bind PS and deliver cytotoxic payload — SN-38 — directly to the tumour microenvironment, without requiring a genetic alteration for patient selection.

04

Built for broad applicability

Because the target is conserved rather than mutation-specific, the platform is designed for expansion across multiple high-PS solid tumour types. PDC-1 begins with colorectal and pancreatic cancer; the platform extends further.

Target Validation

PS exposure is consistent across tumour types

Multiple independent research groups have demonstrated phosphatidylserine exposure on cancer cell membranes across tumour types, regardless of genetic profile — underpinning the pan-tumour hypothesis.

In Vivo Efficacy

Efficacy demonstrated in animal models

New Path's PDC compounds have demonstrated efficacy in animal models of colon and pancreatic cancer, supporting progression of PDC-1 toward IND/CTA-enabling studies.

Safety Signal

No detectable toxicity in preclinical testing

No detectable toxicity has been observed to date across preclinical safety assessments. Formal GLP toxicology studies will be conducted as part of the IND/CTA-enabling programme.

Mechanism Rationale

Synthetic chemistry solves the PS modality problem

PS presents a targeting challenge unsuited to antibodies (no accessible direct binding site) or small molecules (no conventional binding pocket). New Path's synthetic platform addresses this through engineered peptide conjugates — a distinct and defensible approach.
